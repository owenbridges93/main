Erwin Schrödinger


Erwin Rudolf Josef Alexander Schrödinger was an Austrian physicist who won the Nobel-Prize and made great contributions to quantum theory. He is most well known for the postulation of the Schrödinger equation, which facilitates the calculation of the wave function of a system and how it changes over time.


Early Life

Schrödinger was born in August of 1887 in Vienna, Austria. His mother was mixed with Austrian and English descent. His father subscribed to Catholicism and his mother, Lutheranism. Despite this, Schrödinger was an Atheist. He learned to speak English from his grandmother, who was British. Schrödinger studied at the University of Vienna and there he recieved his doctorate.


Middle Life

Schrödinger achieved habilitation in 1914 and went to go participate as a commissioned officer in the Austrian fortress artillery. In 1920 Schrödinger became an associate professor, and a year later, a full professor. In 1933, Schrödinger fled Germany due to his opposition to Nazi antisemitism. While teaching at various universities Schrödinger was awarded a Nobel Prize and proposed the Schrödinger's cat thought experiment.


Late Life and Death

In 1938, the University of Graz dismissed Schrödinger due to political unreliability because of his opposition to Nazism despite a formal statement he issued recanting his opposition. He began visiting positions in other countries' Universities such as Oxford and Ghent University. He later settled down in Ireland where he became a citizen and wrote almost 50 publications on various topics, such as unified field theory. Schrödinger contracted tuberculosis in the late 1920s and it was then that he postulated his wave equation. On the fourth of January, 1961, Schrödinger passed away due to his tuberculosis at age 73. Schrödinger was buried in Alpach, Austria, in a Catholic cemetery.

Major Accomplishments


  1. Postulated the Schrödinger's equation, which governs parts of a quantum mechanical system
  2. Awarded the Nobel Prize of Physics in 1933
  3. Thought of the Schrödinger's cat thought experiment, where a cat is placed in a box, and once a treatment is applied, the cat is both dead and alive
  4. Came up with the Schrödinger method, which is used in combinatorial mathematics and probability theory to solve problems of distribution and occupancy
  5. Determined the existence of quantum energy levels, which are the discrete values of energy that quantum mechanical systems or particles can have
